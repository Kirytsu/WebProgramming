Jakarta Culinary
Kerak Telor
Kerak Telor is a unique and traditional dish characterized by its combination of glutinous rice and eggs, typically cooked over an open flame. This savory omelette is made by spreading a layer of sticky rice in a shallow iron pan, then topping it with beaten eggs, often using duck eggs for a richer flavor. Enhanced with grated coconut and a blend of spices, it offers a delightful taste experience. The dish is usually garnished with crispy fried shallots and can be drizzled with sweet soy sauce before serving. As a popular street food, Kerak Telor showcases a blend of textures and flavors that reflect a rich culinary tradition.
Soto Betawi
Soto Betawi is a traditional Indonesian soup known for its rich and savory flavor. This hearty dish features a fragrant broth made from a blend of spices, including ginger, garlic, and lemongrass, simmered with tender pieces of beef, offal, or chicken. It's typically enriched with coconut milk, giving it a creamy texture and a unique taste. Served with a variety of accompaniments such as sliced tomatoes, fried shallots, and a squeeze of lime, Soto Betawi is often enjoyed with a side of rice or rice cakes, making it a beloved comfort food that reflects a diverse culinary heritage.
Lontong Sayur
Lontong Sayur is a popular dish that consists of compressed rice cakes, known as lontong, served with a flavorful vegetable curry. The dish features a rich, creamy coconut milk broth infused with spices like turmeric, galangal, and lemongrass, which enhances its aromatic qualities. Typically, it includes an assortment of vegetables such as bean sprouts, long beans, and cabbage, often accompanied by boiled eggs and tofu. Garnished with fried shallots and served with sambal for added heat, Lontong Sayur is a delightful combination of textures and flavors, making it a beloved choice for breakfast or as a comforting meal throughout the day.
